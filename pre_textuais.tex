% resumo em inglês
\begin{resumo}[Abstract]
 \begin{otherlanguage*}{english}
    The Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) methodology is widely used by therapists in the treatment of individuals on the autism spectrum, aiming to analyze and reinforce desired behaviors. However, these professionals face challenges in patient management and systematic note-taking. Additionally, family members require effective communication with therapists to support the child's development. This process, essential for the proper application of the ABA method, can be enhanced through a technological tool that facilitates information organization, reduces errors, and increases treatment efficiency. Furthermore, an intuitive interface can strengthen family engagement throughout the therapeutic process.

    This study aims to present an interface proposal that can be implemented in future research. To achieve this, a requirements analysis will be conducted to identify the essential features of the solution. Subsequently, the application interface will be developed based on these requirements and design principles. The purpose of this work is to provide an accessible tool for both therapists and family members, enabling more efficient monitoring of individuals with autism and, consequently, promoting their structured and effective progress.
 \end{otherlanguage*}
\end{resumo}
